6. Once you have completed your website, you are ready to make it official! To make it an official website, you need a web hositing service. This website's web hosting service is Github, and I will show you how to use it. You first need to create an account, and in that accout, create a username. Note that this username will be part of your final url. On the top right corner, you should see a plus sign. Click on it, and then click 'New Resporitory.' Then, in the field that asks for the repository name, insert what you want the name of the resporitory to be. This will be part of the final url. This is what the final url will look like: username.github.io/respository-name. After that, scroll down and click 'Click Resporitory.' Then, you need to upload your HTML file to the resporitory. Find the light blue box that says 'Quick Setup.' Underneath this, there should be a button that lets you upload an existing file. When it takes you to the next screen, click the blue button that kets you choose a file. After you do, scroll down and click 'Commit Changes.' After you have finished that, look towards the top of the page. There should be a settings button. Click on it. There is a menu on the left. The last option in the section 'Codes and automation' is a button called 'Pages.' Click on it. You should see a bolded word 'Branch' somewhere on the page after you click Pages. Underneath it, there should be a drop-down menu leading button that currently says 'None.' Click on it, and click the option 'main.' After that, click 'Save.' After a few minutes, at the top of your page, it will let you know that your website is live, and it will give you the url. If it shows as an error, you may have to repeat the website-creating process a couple of times.
